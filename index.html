Soccer Digest: Interesting facts before the FIFA World Cup

Ahead of the awaited international soccer competition taking place in Qatar in November 2022, this visualisation aims to brush up on some topics that will not go unnoticed to the average soccer fan.

World Cup (WC) Popularity

Sports is one of, if not the most exciting platforms in the entertainment industry. According to a recent survey (July 2022), the international Men's soccer world cup is amongst the most popular sports event in the world, garnering about 3.3 Billion viewers. With massive marketing and adverstising being carried out across the world, the number of viewers is expected to keep increasing.

The World Cup is hosted every 4 years by a country which has been voted by a committee. Below is a map showing the countries which have hosted this beautiful competition and when (if any).

Growing interest for the World Cup

In the early years of this competition, there were not as many matches as there are today. In 1930, only 18 games were played. It was not until the 1998 World Cup that the current world cup format of 64 games has been introduced. This, on top of increasing accessibility to watch soccer games, explains the gradual increase of spectators over the years.


It is interesting to observe from the line chart that there were no World Cups held in 1942 and 1946. Do you know why? This is because those 2 World Cups have been cancelled due to World War 2 and its aftermath.

The World Cup is the highest stage for top performing countries to compete against each other. Brazil has the most number of WC wins in history and is considered to be one of the favorites to win the upcoming tournament. Germany with 4 WC wins and Copa America 2021 Winner Argentina are both considered serious contenders too.

Bubble plot illustrates contenders' performances in the history of WCs.

Goal Galore in WCs

Most memorable World Cup moments almost all generate from the spectacular goals which are scored throughout this competition.

The box and whisker plot on the right demonstrates an overview of how previous World Cup winners fared in these competitions and offers some interesting insights.

For example, 4 times winner Italy may not be the most spectacular offensively scoring an average number of 10 goals per world cup, but still manage to be neck-to-neck with Germany in terms of WC wins.

However, scoring goals is not all that is the most important. It is crucial for teams to have a solid defence, i.e concede least amount of goals as possible. The box and whisker plot and bubble plots can be analysed in conjunction to help you predict your 2022 World Cup Winner.
